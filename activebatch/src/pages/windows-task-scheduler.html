A centralized scheduler application that streamlines job scheduling across Windows and non-Windows systems. faqs: What is workload automation?: Workload automation (WLA) is the practice of using software to streamline, schedule and manage complex processes, applications and tasks across an organization\'s IT ecosystem. It enables users to automate manual, repetitive processes and ensures that interdependent workflows are executed efficiently and consistently, reducing the need for manual human intervention. WLA platforms with intuitive user interfaces allow for no-code/low-code workflow development for DevOps, data management and much more. Discover the capabilities workload automation products offer for IT automation, ETL and more. What is the difference between job scheduling and workload automation?: Job scheduling and workload automation solutions were designed for different goals, but they\'re closely related and share some functionality. Job scheduling tools focus on individual tasks or “jobs” at a specific time or in a defined sequence. These jobs are often standalone and have minimal dependencies. They are often triggered by time-based schedules. Workload automation (WLA) goes beyond individual jobs to orchestrate interconnected workflows that span multiple systems or applications. WLA handles complex, event-driven workflows that adapt based on real-time conditions, such as new emails or Google Calendar events. WLA software features dashboards that allow users to track progress for multiple jobs and workflows from a single point of control.
